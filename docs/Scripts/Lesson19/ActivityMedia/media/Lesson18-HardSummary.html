Over 3000 years ago the first Olympic games was held in Greece. During the time that the Olympics were held there was a truce called the 'Olympic Truce' where fighting and wars were halted. In the 19th century a French man brought the Olympics to modern times. To promote peace he encourage athletes from all over the world to come together so that they could understand one another better. In the late 1800's the Olympics Committees was formed with 12 countries attending. Modern Olympics are an international friendship of athletes. The Olympic truce was instated to promote peace among the world's nations and the Olympics give the world a way to celebrate and honor athletes from different countries.
According to legend, the ancient Olympic Games were founded by Heracles (the Roman Hercules), a son of Zeus. Yet the first Olympic Games for which we still have written records were held in 776 BCE (though it is generally believed that the Games had been going on for many years already). At this Olympic Games, a naked runner, Coroebus (a cook from Elis), won the sole event at the Olympics, the stade - a run of approximately 192 meters (210 yards). This made Coroebus the very first Olympic champion in history. The ancient Olympic Games grew and continued to be played every four years for nearly 1200 years. In 393 CE, the Roman emperor Theodosius I, a Christian, abolished the Games because of their pagan influences.

The Olympic Games

Idea
The heart of the Olympic Games is the uniting of countries in peace and sportsmanship to celebrate and honor the finest Olympians from each country.
1. During the Truce, wars were suspended, armies were prohibited from threatening the Games, legal disputes were stopped, and death penalties were forbidden.
2. Coubertin thought that by encouraging athletics for the youth, people of the world would come to have a better understanding of one another.
3. The Olympic Truce was revived by UN resolution 48/11 of October 25th 1993, as well by the United Nations Millennium Declaration relating to world peace and security.
