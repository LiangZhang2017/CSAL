The Legend of the Evening Star
There was, as in the fairy tales, a most beautiful maid of famous royal blood. She was her parents' only child, and her beauty was unparalleled.
Every night from her bedroom window, the princess directed her gaze towards the bright Evening Star. She fell in love with him. And he, the bright Evening Star, also looked upon her as she upon him, and returned his love for her.
1
One night, as the princess lay smiling upon her bed. She spoke to him in her sleep and said:
"O gentle Evening Star, descend to me, enter my chamber and my mind, and shine over my life with your light!"
He listened and quivered. Craving more of her love, he plunged into the sea waves as quickly as lightning. From the depths of the rolling waves, a superb young man sprang forth. A great prince he seemed to be with soft golden hair.
2
But, he possessed a face that appeared to be made of wax, handsome but dead. His eyes were a deep sea blue, but there was no sparkle in them.
"I come as you have called." The Evening Star said. "I am the Evening Star. The heaven is my father and the sea is my mother. O, come my wonderful treasure and leave your world with me as my bride. In my coral palace, you will live forever, and the entire world of the sea will worship you!"
3
And the princess answered: "O, you are beautiful, but only in my dreams are you the angel for me. For I am alive and you are dead, and your eyes chill my heart."
Days passed and the Evening Star stayed where he was in the sky, as he always had. In her sleep, the princess was reminded of him and called to him once again. The Evening Star came down at her plea. He asked her to be his bride once more. But, she rejected him yet again.
4
The Evening Star prince then said: "In what way would you have me then? I cannot hide the fact that I am an immortal, whereas you are a thing that must die!"
And the princess answered: "If you want my love, then you must come down to this temporal earth, and be as mortal as I am."
The Evening Star prince agreed to her request. To fulfill the wish of this beloved mortal, he plucked himself from the sky and was gone for many days.
5
Meanwhile in the palace, a servant boy named Catalin snuck like a thief to the rooms where the princess was. Her name was Catalina and he was overwhelmed by her beauty. He decided to woo her. When he showed himself to Catalina and asked for a kiss, she told him to stay away from her. She told him that she loved only the Evening Star.
Catalin said to Catalina: "You are such a child! Let us go and lose ourselves in new lands and you will no longer long for the Evening Star!"
6
So, what had happened to the Evening Star during all this time?
The Evening Star flew into the heavens and traveled for thousands of years in a flash. He came to an eternal void and said unto his Creator: "Hear my prayer, O my Lord and Father. You who give life also give death. Take back my endlessness, and give me a chance to love as humans do, even for just one hour!"
He was answered: "Evening Star, my divine child, do not ask for nonsensical things...
7
You wish to be a man rather than a star? Men die and are born quickly. Each living thing lives only because it dies. You, my Evening Star, will be the wonder of all time in the sky where you will stay. Why do you ask to be mortal, instead? I can give you dominions over all land and sea, but I cannot give you death. Why don't you go and see if what you have asked for on earth is worth this sacrifice?"
The Evening Star went straight away to the night sky, flying back through many ages.
8
He came to an evening in a forest, where beneath the tall trees, a young couple sat together and kissed.
The Evening Star gazed down and beheld how the young couple loved each other. He heard the young woman as she told her young man: "O, my darling, you are my first love and also my last dream!"
Even as she was bewitched by love, she lifted her eyes toward the starry night, and, upon witnessing the Evening Star, she called to him with desire once more: "Descend to me, gentle Evening Star, enter my forest and my mind, and shine over my good fortunes!"
9
This time, the Evening Star did not fall as before, but cried from his heart: "What do you care, specks of dust, whether you have me or someone else? You live in your narrow sphere and are ruled by your fortunes. In my steady world, I will stay eternally cold and true!"
