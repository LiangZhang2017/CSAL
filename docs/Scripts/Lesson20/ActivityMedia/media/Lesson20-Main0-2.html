Minor Burns
Minor burns are a relatively common problem in many places like in the home or at work. There are many things you should and should not do when someone has experienced a minor burn. One thing you want to do is cool the burn. Hold the burned area under cool (not cold) running water for 10 to 15 minutes or until the pain subsides. You don't want to use ice or ice water because it can cause a person's body to become too cold and cause further damage to the wound. If pain persists, you can take an over-the-counter pain reliever. Over-the-counter means it is a medicine that you do not need a prescription to buy. Cover the burn with a sterile gauze bandage. Bandaging keeps air off the burn, reduces pain and protects blistered skin. Protecting the burn is important. Watch for signs of infection. These signs include: increased pain, redness, fever, swelling, or oozing. You shouldn't apply egg whites, butter, or ointments to the burn because they could cause infection. If infection develops, seek medical help.
