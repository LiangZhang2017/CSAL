Little Herds
Little Herds is an Austin nonprofit organization focused on educating the next generation on the environmental and nutritional benefits of edible insects. Our purpose is to work with schools, businesses, and community members to promote this sustainable way of feeding not only ourselves, but the world.
Who Eats Bugs?
Countless cultures around the world eat insects. Studies suggest that up to 80% of the world's population has eaten insects! People eat them in Central and South America, Africa, Australia, India, China, Japan - the list goes on.

Eco-Friendly
Insects can be raised in almost any climate or environment, and they require less land, water, feed, and energy than traditional protein sources. Some scientists are even experimenting with growing them in space! Even better, insects don't harbor diseases transmittable to humans, like mad cow disease or swine and avian flu.
Cost-Efficient
Insects can convert feed to human-consumable protein more efficiently than traditional livestock like beef, and they need less energy and less space to grow. From crickets to mealworms and beyond, insects provide a large return with little input, especially compared with traditional livestock.
Tasty!
There are 2,000 species of insects known to be edible, and tens of thousands yet to be tested. Insects can be prepared in a variety of ways and used in many diverse dishes. Western chefs are just discovering the art of entomophagy. Every day people are trying new and delicious ways to serve bugs!
Our Bugs are Safe , Clean, and All-Natural
Since April 2012, Little Herds had collaborated with World Entomophagy to provide high-quality, human-consumption grade insects. World Ento is the first company growing, processing, and distributing these insects on an industrial scale. The hygienic conditions, stringent health standards, and high-quality feed make World Ento's insects top-notch in both flavor and safety. Little Herds has been growing mealworms in Austin since March 2013. We have used World Ento as a standard for our insects to assure that we are providing the highest quality insect possible.
OUR PLANS:
Bug Dinners
Little Herds is partnering with local restaurants to provide ongoing opportunities for people to try insects prepared by their favorite chefs in familiar and comfortable settings. These dinners will include educational presentations and discussions on entomophagy.
School Presentations
Little Herds is interested in working with local schools, from elementary to college. We will bring educational presentations on why insects are nutritious, as well as how to grow and cook your own insects. We are also partnering with sister schools in other countries.
Farmers Markets
Little Herds is working with local farmers markets, including HOPE, to provide educational materials and edible insect samples.
Festivals
Little Herds will appear at local festivals to help promote healthy eating habits and sustainable farming practices.
